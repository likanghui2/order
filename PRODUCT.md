# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Operations staff who create, monitor, and troubleshoot local airline seat-holding tasks. They work in a dense task console, often under time pressure, and need to scan many records without losing context.

## Product Purpose

Local Sham Booking Runner provides a desktop-like command workspace for creating booking tasks, importing batches, managing PNR records, configuring source proxies, and reviewing execution results stored in local SQLite. Success means operators can find the right task or PNR quickly, trust current state, and take the next action with low friction.

## Brand Personality

High-energy command desk, precise, calm, operational.

## Anti-references

This should not look like a marketing landing page, a decorative analytics demo, or a dark cockpit that sacrifices readability. Avoid oversized hero sections, ornamental cards, novelty motion, weak contrast, and horizontal table sprawl unless a workflow truly requires it.

## Design Principles

- Keep the current workflow in view: filters, tables, logs, and actions should stay close to the operator's task.
- Optimize for dense scanning: rows, labels, badges, and timestamps need predictable alignment and stable widths.
- Preserve command confidence: controls should feel direct, fast, and unambiguous.
- Prefer useful restraint over decoration: visual polish should clarify hierarchy, state, and risk.
- Treat accessibility as operating reliability: target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-friendly controls, and readable states.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Primary text, labels, placeholders, badges, and table states should maintain accessible contrast. Interfaces should remain usable with keyboard navigation, reduced motion preferences, and color-blind-safe status communication.
